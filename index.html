Scientific Employee

at the Leibniz Institute for Plant Genetics and Crop Plant Research IPK Gatersleben
Department of Physiology and Cell Biology / Applied Biochemistry & Department Molecular Genetics / Network Analysis and Modelling

Project: BARLEY BIODIVERSITY - Increasing biodiversity and productivity of barley cultivars using a sensor-based high-throughput phenotyping system in the field and association mapping with two wild barley introgression libraries

April 2020 - December 2020

The project was part of the initiative „Innovative Pflanzenzüchtung im Anbausystem“ (IPAS)

Tasks and methods: My task in the project was the evaluation and statistical analysis of multi-modal data from a large barley maping population. The data have been obtained using various analytical techniques and across several years. In terms of analytical complexity, the project included handling large and diverse data structures, including: genotype data, mass spectrometry profiles, phenotype descriptors and quantitative phenotypes, as well as analysis of the data variation and reproducibility across different cultivation years and plant developmental stages.


Analysis of metabolic data: Identification of metabolic features in the mass spectrometry data obtained by HPLC-MS/MS, providing a comprehensive profile of semi-polar

compounds accumulated in barley leaves

Data normalization:Data normalization using multiple internal standards by fitting a multivariate linear model to a set of reference

internal standards and removing the fitted variance from the target variables.

Quality control: Quality control (QC) samples were used to estimate the data reproducibility and technical error across analytical batches and cultivation seasons.

ANOVA: Analysis of variance has been performed to highlight effects of different experimental factors: e.g. genotype, batch, developmental stage.

Pearson correlation analysis: Correlation analysis has been performed between measured variables using Pearson’s correlation coefficient. Homogeneity of the correlations across cultivation seasons has been analysed and stable correlations have been identified.

Cluster determination using GAP statistics: Cross validation-based methods for cluster number estimation have been applied to estimate the number of stable clusters in each dataset and cultivation year.

Regularized regression: Regularized regression has been used for feature selection and phenotype prediction, including ridge regression, elastic net and LASSO.

Software used in frame of the project: R, Excel, Compass DataAnalysis, QuantAnalysis and MetaboScape (Bruker, Bremen)

The work will be submitted for publication, hopefully within a few months.


Project: Integration of biotic and abiotic stress responses in barley

at the Department of Physiology and Cell Biology / Applied Biochemistry

June 2018 - March 2020

The project aimed on understanding the coordination of the stress responses of barley. My task was to characterize cellular reactions of a diverse barley population to a biotic and abiotic stressor. As abiotic stress the plants were exposed to cold and light. Biotic stress was a pathogenic fungi.

To evaluate the metabolic response of a variety of barley to different kinds of stress, semi-polar compounds were analyzed using UPLC-MS/MS. Enzymatic and non-enzymatic components of the oxidative stress response were analyzed. For non-invasive characterization, parameters of the photosynthesis were measured as fluorescence signals of the leaves.

Master Thesis

at the Fraunhofer Institute for Molecular Biology and Applied Ecology IME Aachen
Title: Exploration of pseudogenes related to disease resistance in potato

April 2017 - Dezember 2017

Abstract: Pseudogenes have mostly been considered to be useless leftovers of once functional genes. However, a new view emerged when pseudogenes were discovered to have beneficial properties. One aspect is the perception of pseudogenes acting as a storage for genetic diversity from which novel functions can arise. This observation led to the notion of resistance gene-like pseudogenes as a source for new resistances. Pseudogenes are non-functional due to premature stop codons or frameshift mutations. The pre-sent thesis is part of a research project, which seeks to identify these genetic degenerations and restore the full-length protein by alterations on the single nucleotide level. The present work aims to contribute to the identification of pseudogenes by validating the presence of selected pseudogenes and characterizing their regulatory regions. Potato was chosen for this approach due to its high importance as a food crop and great efforts in resistance breeding to reduce crop losses. We focused on two pseudogenes, which were validated by PCR amplification and sequencing. Subsequently, we located the putative promoter region and assessed its activity. The intent of the promoter identification was to use the native promoter to regulate the altered pseudogene after restoration. The putative promoter of one selected pseudogene was identified by PCR walking and sequencing. The region was inserted into a vec-tor upstream of the reporter gene DsRed. To test the promoter activity, Nicotiana benthamiana was transformed with the construct. We observed local expression of DsRed, indicating promoter activity. However, the findings were difficult to reproduce, so that at the current time, no certain statements can be made as to whether the observed promoter activity is inducible by specific

conditions.

Bachelor Thesis

at Rheinisch Westfälische Technische Hochschule RWTH university hospital
Institute for Hygiene and Environmental Health
Title: Bioaerosols from pig fattening – Characterization and Quantification of airborne microorganisms

April 2015 - September 2015

Abstract: Bioaerosols can cause a wide spectrum of adverse health effects. In animal husbandry airborne microorganisms are found in high concentrations and thus represent a major health risk for employees. In this paper, microorganisms from pig fattening farms were characterized and quantified to assess the microflora from pig facilities. In addition, concentrations outside the facility were determined to detect possible pollution in the surroundings. The concentrations of total bacteria, total fungi and staphylococci were found to be suitable indicators for assessing the microbial load in swine barns. No influence could be detected outside the enclosure. Impingement and filtration were used methods for sampling. For bacteria Impingement yielded a higher biological collection efficiency than the filtration procedure. Filtration proved to be more suitable for detection of mold fungi.
